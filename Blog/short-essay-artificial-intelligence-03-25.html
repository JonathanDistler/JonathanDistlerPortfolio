I’ve been thinking about this topic a lot and really trying to weigh the pros and cons of using AI. I think that it comes down to two main trains of thought: process versus outcome. I think that AI is truly a productivity multiplier when doing anything with respect to what I consider tedious work (e.g. drafting emails, finding technical specifications for a project, or combing over multiple websites at the click of a single prompt). It’s oftentimes quicker, easier, and less mental overhead to enter a quick thought or question into the “Ask Anything” tab within ChatGPT rather than look over the internet; refer to more traditional means like advice from family or well trusted peers, or even -god forbid- trusting your own judgement on a matter. During my time as a student, I think that my interest in most topics and my breadth of knowledge within almost all of my courses has diminished as a result of using generative AI tools in a regular capacity, however, my output has increased almost without bound.

The new status quo has been to let these tools permeate every aspect of one’s life, whether that be asking almost trivial questions (“Is it okay to wear vertical and horizontal stripes at the same time?”) or asking meaningful technical questions (“What is the difference between a stream function and a potential flow?”), I would know because I’ve asked both of these questions within the last week. Most of these questions are not one-off use cases, where you can use the output once and disregard it for the foreseeable future, having truly thought through the question and weighed the pros and cons, looked through different reference materials and really synthesized a legitimate answer is going to stick with one much longer than the half-assed prompt and quick skim over the uncanny output text. Spending the extra 10-15 minutes to really overhaul the knowledge on the subject is going to save hours in the future by not needing to re-prompt engineer the same question because there will be an empirical solution.

I think this short-sighted solution favoring outputs to process has also corroded much of what was left of our social fabric that had already been going out of vogue after the Covid-19 pandemic. I can remember asking my parents about what it meant to dress “business casually”, now my first instinct is to ask Chat GPT and take whatever it says as a ground truth; granted my parents probably wouldn’t appreciate a phone-call every time I had a question like the latter, but calling them with more frequency to ask more pertinent questions would keep us in touch more, distill more wisdom to me, and really improve a lot of the relationships that were often taken for granted. Chat GPT, is usually right (or at least right in most regards), but I think that the idea that the outcome matters more than the process will really have severe consequences that even the most foresighted individuals can’t forecast. I think that favoring short-term output over long-term process relations will dissolve much of the joys of learning and will favor the more opportunistic students who use these tools without regard for their unintentional losses as a result.

That is why I’m making a pledge to avoid using generative AI tools in all capacities at a possible loss of throughput but at an increase in my critical thinking skills and as a testament to perseverance, after all I am named after “John Henry the Steel Driving Man”, someone who went toe-to-toe with the world’s foremost technology and came out on top due to his perseverance and tenacity coupled with the innate human dive for greatness. (This was not written by generative AI).
